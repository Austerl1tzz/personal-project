Understanding Double Angle Formula

The Double Angle Formula is a trigonometric identity that expresses trigonometric functions of double angles (e.g., sin(2θ), cos(2θ), and tan(2θ)) in terms of the functions of θ.

Formula Overview

The Double Angle Formula has three main forms:

  • sin(2θ) = 2sin(θ)cos(θ)
  • cos(2θ) = cos²(θ) - sin²(θ)
  • tan(2θ) = 2tan(θ) / (1 - tan²(θ))

These identities are derived from the sum of angles formula for sine, cosine, and tangent.

Examples

Example 1:

Problem: Find sin(2θ) if sin(θ) = 3/5 and cos(θ) = 4/5.

Solution:

Step 1: Use the formula sin(2θ) = 2sin(θ)cos(θ).

Step 2: Substitute the values of sin(θ) and cos(θ): sin(2θ) = 2(3/5)(4/5) = 24/25.

Answer: sin(2θ) = 24/25

Example 2:

Problem: Find cos(2θ) if cos(θ) = 1/3 and sin(θ) = √8/3.

Solution:

Step 1: Use the formula cos(2θ) = cos²(θ) - sin²(θ).

Step 2: Substitute the values: cos(2θ) = (1/3)² - (√8/3)² = 1/9 - 8/9 = -7/9.

Answer: cos(2θ) = -7/9

Example 3:

Problem: Find tan(2θ) if tan(θ) = 1/2.

Solution:

Step 1: Use the formula tan(2θ) = 2tan(θ) / (1 - tan²(θ)).

Step 2: Substitute the value of tan(θ): tan(2θ) = 2(1/2) / (1 - (1/2)²) = 1 / (1 - 1/4) = 1 / (3/4) = 4/3.

Answer: tan(2θ) = 4/3

Graph of Double Angle Functions

The graphs of the double angle functions display periodic behavior, but with a frequency twice that of their single angle counterparts. These graphs are useful in analyzing oscillatory motion and waveforms.
